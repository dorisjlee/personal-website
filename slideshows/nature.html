Some hanksite samples collected from Searle's Lake in Trona, California

Other salt mineralization on surface to be cleaned off.

Hanksite is like salt, so dissolves in water. Since I didn't have an actual hard steel brush, I dabbled a bit of water for dissolving away the surface mineral.

The translucent mineral looks really cool and worth the mud and scrub.

Sulfur found near a deserted sulfur mine in Death Valley, California.

A larger sample.

This piece of Obsidian is found near the Crowley Lake columns. Obsidians are volcanic lava that cooled too fast to crystalize,resulting in this very glassy smooth surface.

Pine cones collected in Berkeley's campus in 2014.

Pinecones collected while hiking to Telescope Peak.

Maybe because of the Mojhave desert weather conditions, these pinecones are a lot flakier than the more wooden-like ones in Berkeley.
